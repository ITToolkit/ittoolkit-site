ITToolkit Privacy Policy

Clear, simple information about what the app accesses and why

Last Updated: 8 March 2026

In plain English: ITToolkit is a utility app. Most data stays on your device. The app accesses network, Wi‑Fi, notifications, and location-related information only to power features you choose to use. It also stores some information locally on your device, such as lookup history, saved hosts, SSL watchlists, settings, and Pro status.

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how ITToolkit handles information when you use the app.

ITToolkit is designed for network and troubleshooting tasks such as DNS lookups, port checks, traceroute, SSL inspection, HTTP inspection, Wi‑Fi analysis, DNS propagation checks, CSV export, and optional Pro features.

By using ITToolkit, you agree to this Privacy Policy.

2. What ITToolkit Accesses

Depending on which features you use, ITToolkit may access the following categories of information:

  • Network and internet information, such as hostnames, domains, IP addresses, URLs, open ports, certificate details, resolver responses, and network status, in order to perform the tool you requested.
  • Wi‑Fi and nearby network information, such as SSID, BSSID, signal strength, frequency, and scan results, when you use the Wi‑Fi scanner.
  • Location-related information, including your device’s current location and reverse-geocoded area details, only when you use Wi‑Fi features that require Android location access.
  • App usage and settings, such as onboarding completion, dark mode preference, custom DNS preference, free usage counters, and Pro purchase state.
  • User-entered content, such as hosts, domains, URLs, saved bookmarks, lookup history, SSL watchlist entries, and exported CSV content.

ITToolkit does not ask for contacts, microphone, camera, photos, SMS, or background location access.

3. What Is Stored Locally on Your Device

ITToolkit stores certain information locally on your device so app features work properly. This may include:

  • Lookup history and tool results you choose to run
  • Saved hosts and bookmarks
  • SSL watchlist entries for expiry alerts
  • App settings and preferences
  • Free tool usage counts and Pro unlock status
  • CSV files you export to your device’s Downloads folder

This local information is stored on your device and is not sold by us.

4. Permissions Used by the App

Internet Access

Used to perform network tools such as DNS lookups, port checks, traceroute, SSL checks, HTTP inspection, DNS propagation checks, speed tests, Google Play billing, and in-app review.

Network State and Wi‑Fi State

Used to detect connection status, read current Wi‑Fi details, and support Wi‑Fi scanning and related diagnostics.

Precise and Approximate Location

Android requires location permission for Wi‑Fi scanning. ITToolkit also uses location in the Wi‑Fi screen to show current coordinates and reverse-geocoded area information on your device. This access is feature-driven and only used when relevant app tools are opened or run.

Notifications

Used for optional SSL certificate expiry alerts for domains you add to the watchlist.

5. How Information Is Used

ITToolkit uses accessed information only to provide the features you choose to use, including:

  • Running diagnostic and networking tools you request
  • Displaying nearby Wi‑Fi and current network information
  • Saving history, bookmarks, and watchlists on your device
  • Creating CSV exports on your device
  • Sending optional SSL expiry notifications
  • Enabling Pro purchase and restore functionality through Google Play
  • Allowing you to submit an in-app review through Google Play

6. Network Requests and External Services

Because ITToolkit is a network utility app, it sends requests from your device to the systems needed to complete the tool you selected. For example:

  • DNS and DNS propagation tools contact DNS resolver endpoints
  • HTTP Inspector connects to the URL you enter
  • SSL Checker connects to the host you enter
  • Port Scan and Traceroute connect to the host or IP you enter
  • Wi‑Fi speed test uses remote test endpoints
  • Pro purchase and restore use Google Play Billing
  • In-app review uses Google Play review services

These network requests are necessary for the app to function. The systems you query may receive standard connection metadata from your device, such as IP address and request details, as part of normal internet communication.

ITToolkit itself is not designed to sell your data or use it for advertising.

7. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal data.

We do not intentionally share your data with third parties for advertising purposes.

However, when you use app tools that connect to external systems, data is sent directly from your device to the relevant destination needed for the tool to work, such as a host you entered, a DNS resolver, or Google Play services for purchases and reviews.

8. Data Retention

Data stored locally in ITToolkit remains on your device until you delete it, clear app data, uninstall the app, or remove it through app controls where available.

Exported CSV files remain in your device storage until you delete them.

9. Security

We use reasonable measures within the app to protect locally stored data and app functionality. However, no device, network connection, or internet transmission can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

10. Your Rights (UK GDPR)

If you are in the United Kingdom, you may have rights under UK data protection law, including the right to request access, correction, deletion, or restriction of personal data where applicable.

Because ITToolkit mainly stores information locally on your device and is designed to minimise developer-held data, these rights may be limited in practice unless you contact us about specific support-related information.

11. Children’s Privacy

ITToolkit is not directed to children under 13 and is not designed for child use.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the “Last Updated” date at the top of this page.
